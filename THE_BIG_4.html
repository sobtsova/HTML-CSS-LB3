The "Big Four" of thrash metal—Metallica, Slayer, Megadeth, and Anthrax—are considered the most influential bands in the genre. Their groundbreaking albums set new standards for speed, complexity, and lyrical themes.

Formed in the early 1980s, the "Big Four" of thrash metal rose from the gritty underground scene to become global icons of heavy metal. They pushed the boundaries of speed, aggression, and lyrical content, creating a raw and intense style that set them apart from traditional heavy metal. Not only did they pioneer thrash metal’s unique sound, but they also helped shape the genre’s identity, introducing it to mainstream audiences.

The "Big Four" bands revolutionized thrash metal with their raw energy and powerful stage presence. Together, they shaped the face of modern metal music.
